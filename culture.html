CULTURE

Language: Arabic is the official language of Sudan, reflecting the historical influence of Arab cultures. Additionally, there are many indigenous languages spoken across the country, including Nubian, Beja, Fur, and others. Religion: Islam is the dominant religion in Sudan, and the majority of the population is Sunni Muslim. The practice of Islam has a significant impact on various aspects of Sudanese culture, including daily life, art, and architecture.Sudanese music is diverse, with various traditional genres reflecting the cultural diversity of the country. Instruments like the oud, drums, and traditional string instruments are commonly used. Traditional dances are an essential part of Sudanese cultural expression. Different regions have distinct dance styles, often performed during celebrations, ceremonies, and social gatherings.
